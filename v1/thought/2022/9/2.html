I thought it'd be nice to tell how I discovered music and how it's going. At least something fun to type about while school ends. We get a 3 day weekend yay..
I've always been fascinated with music ever since I was a little kid. Though very dumb as I only ever listened to Sonic the Hedghehog soundtracks. Specifically Sonic 1 and 2. I would sit down and just listen to the tracks as if they were normal songs. I was probably about 6-8. The first change in my music journey was when I was around 9 or 10, as I started to listen more of Legend of Zelda tracks. Haha nothing different, just a different game.
The genuine breakthough came when I was 11 or 12. I had a girlfriend who's initials I'll refer to K.C. She was very much into older bands and if I recall correctly, loved The Beatles, KISS, and The Rolling Stones. Keep a note of The Beatles, as they will be a legitimate crucial part of my life. She had been telling me to listen to The Beatles for a while now, and while I refused since I didn't want to stop listening to video game tracks, I, at last, gave in. I was playing "Doomspire Brickbattle" on Roblox, and my dad had bought YouTube Premium, which included YouTube music. I decided to search up The Beatles as I was curious and came across "Hey Jude". I reluctantly clicked on it, and listened. I wouldn't say it was "love at first hearing", but it was very close. I fell in love with the "Naaaaa, Naaa, Naaa, Na Na Na Na"'s at the end and listened to some more songs. It took a bit, but I now had a small collection of songs I liked from The Beatles. My girlfriend and I weren't together at that point, but over the next months, I'd find myself listening to The Beatles more and more and more! It took a little over a year and a half until I realized they had albums, and it took 2 or 3 to finally listen to all of them and like them! I only listen to The Beatles now, soo thank you ex girlfriend for introducing them to me haha. The Beatles were the ones who got me to actually want to make music, and so I've been playing small amounts of piano.
